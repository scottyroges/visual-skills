Domain

Six role-based AI brains that perceive the league through their own noisy lens and decide what their franchise does.

OwnsAll strategic planning, player evaluation, satisfaction, blame, and arc decisions for the GM, Coach, Owner, Player, Scout, and Agent roles.
WhyThere is no god-mode AI. Each role sees a skill-scaled, noise-distorted version of the truth, so a weak GM genuinely mis-values its own roster — and that is where franchise dysfunction comes from.
RuleBrains never mutate world state. They read the world and return decisions; lib/sim executes them. That one-way contract keeps the season deterministic.
The load-bearing idea
“Skill is perception quality, not decision quality.”

Every brain runs the same perceive → assess → decide pipeline. What differs by skill is the noise added during perception: evaluatePlayer adds Gaussian error scaled by (100 − evaluatorSkill). High-skill GMs see clearly; low-skill GMs over-value other teams’ assets and under-value their own.

Internal architecture

perceive → decide

Every brain runs the same pipeline. The trigger engine reads world state, the perception layer distorts it by skill, role assessments cache a weekly snapshot, and decisions are handed to sim to execute.

World state (read-only)PerceptionThis domainsim (executes)
☉Assessments are cached with a 7-day TTL (AssessmentCache), so each role re-perceives the league about once a week rather than every day — cheap, and “fresh enough” to drive decisions.

The brains, in depth

6 roles

Each brain, what it actually computes, and how it connects to the rest of the system.

gm lib/brain/gm

↑ back to cards

The GM is the deepest brain. Beyond a weekly GMAssessment (roster depth, position gaps, window state, posture), it maintains a perceived 5-year StrategicPlan — a CapBoard (ceiling, committed, dead cap, perceived flexibility), an OrgDepthChart (factual slots + perceived tiers), a PipelineMap (prospect readiness), and a ProScoutingBoard (tiered FA / deadline targets). Crucially the plan is perceived: a low-skill GM mis-tiers its own depth chart and chases the wrong targets.

The plan isn’t rebuilt from scratch each tick. When a big event fires — a star injury, a prospect breakout, an owner mandate change, a rival blockbuster — a disruption handler registry computes a PlanDelta and applies it in place, then cascades follow-up triggers (rebuild the cap board after a target is removed, etc.). In-season cap and roster events fire fireReplanTrigger directly.

triggerthe plan

Its other headline path is the weekly trade cycle, where perception asymmetry turns into real value transfer between teams.

coach lib/brain/coach

↑ back to cards

The coach rates players by computeSystemFitRating — not raw skill, but fit to its system — then buildLineup assembles four forward lines, three D pairs, PP/PK units, and a starting goalie, balancing chemistry. recommendScratches flags underperformers, which the GM must approve before they sit. A separate staff review evaluates assistant-coach effectiveness.

Connections
producesa lineup consumed by sim each game; scratch recommendations gated by the GM
readsRosterSOA, chemistry, and player points (sim); CoachProfile (profiles)
emitsfriction tags (e.g. locker_room_unrest) to story

owner lib/brain/owner

↑ back to cards

The owner perceives wins and losses clearly but roster quality poorly (fixed low skill ~30). Each month it runs evaluateOwnerBlame to attribute losing to the coach, the GM, or the roster, and refreshes a rolling satisfaction metric for each of the coach and GM. Blame and firing both debounce — a target must persist across two cycles before it sticks — which suppresses single-cycle RNG noise.

player lib/brain/player

↑ back to cards

computeSelfAssessment rates a player’s own skill with ego-driven inflation or deflation; computeSatisfaction blends ice time, team success, coach fit, city, and contract fairness into a PlayerSatisfaction. Low satisfaction plus an inflated self-view drives contract demands and trade-request flags. A separate path handles archetype assignment and retirement.

Connections
producesdemands & wantsTradeRequest flags — read by the gm and by story arcs
readsown roster row, ice time, and contract terms (sim / world)

scout lib/brain/scout

↑ back to cards

buildIndividualReport produces a per-prospect read — ceiling and current skill, each blurred by Gaussian noise scaled by the scout’s accuracy and region specialty — and buildDraftBoard aggregates the team’s scouts into a ranked board. The board is the GM’s perceived view of the draft class, so a weak scouting department systematically mis-ranks prospects.

Connections
producesDraftBoard — consumed by the gm at the draft
readsdraft-eligible prospects (world), scaled by ScoutProfile accuracy + region (profiles)

agent lib/brain/agent

↑ back to cards

The narrowest brain: pickAgentDecision chooses a negotiation stance for a player’s contract when a story arc surfaces a decision (e.g. agent_negotiation_stance). Like the other roles’ arc deciders, it scores known options and falls back to a weighted pick, tagging the result ai_brain or placeholder for audit.

Connections
invoked bystory — via pickArcOption when an arc decision is the agent’s

Data it owns

Brain state is its own; world state is borrowed. These are the types lib/brain defines and persists across the season.

BrainStateCross-season state: strategic plans, disruption logs, and walkdown (replan) audit events.
SeasonBrainState · TeamBrainStatePer-season working state: assessment caches, satisfaction, blame, and firing & trade records.
GMAssessment · CoachAssessment · OwnerAssessmentCached weekly perception snapshots (7-day TTL via AssessmentCache), one per role per team.
StrategicPlanThe GM’s perceived 5-year plan: CapBoard, OrgDepthChart, PipelineMap, ProScoutingBoard, draft board.
TradeProposal · TradeEvaluationThe trade negotiation loop: proposals, perceived valuations, accept/reject/counter outcomes.

Reads (never owns): RosterSOA, standings, cap state from lib/sim; Franchise/Person from lib/world; profiles & personality from lib/profiles.

Domain

Six role-based AI brains that perceive the league through their own noisy lens and decide what their franchise does.

OwnsAll strategic planning, player evaluation, satisfaction, blame, and arc decisions for the GM, Coach, Owner, Player, Scout, and Agent roles.
WhyThere is no god-mode AI. Each role sees a skill-scaled, noise-distorted version of the truth, so a weak GM genuinely mis-values its own roster — and that is where franchise dysfunction comes from.
RuleBrains never mutate world state. They read the world and return decisions; lib/sim executes them. That one-way contract keeps the season deterministic.
The load-bearing idea
“Skill is perception quality, not decision quality.”

Every brain runs the same perceive → assess → decide pipeline. What differs by skill is the noise added during perception: evaluatePlayer adds Gaussian error scaled by (100 − evaluatorSkill). High-skill GMs see clearly; low-skill GMs over-value other teams’ assets and under-value their own.

Internal architecture

perceive → decide

Every brain runs the same pipeline. The trigger engine reads world state, the perception layer distorts it by skill, role assessments cache a weekly snapshot, and decisions are handed to sim to execute.

World state (read-only)PerceptionThis domainsim (executes)
☉Shared infrastructure: perception.ts (evaluatePlayer, computeLeagueAverages, AssessmentCache) and triggers.ts (runDayTriggers, runPostGameTriggers) are used by every role below. Assessments cache with a 7-day TTL, so each role re-perceives the league about once a week.

The brains, in depth

6 roles

A full section per brain — what it computes, how it works, its files and exports, and how it connects to the rest of the system.

gm lib/brain/gm

↑ back to cards

The GM is the deepest brain. Beyond a weekly GMAssessment (roster depth, position gaps, window state, posture), it maintains a perceived 5-year StrategicPlan — a CapBoard (ceiling, committed, dead cap, perceived flexibility), an OrgDepthChart (factual slots + perceived tiers), a PipelineMap (prospect readiness), and a ProScoutingBoard (tiered FA / deadline targets). Crucially the plan is perceived: a low-skill GM mis-tiers its own depth chart and chases the wrong targets.

The plan isn’t rebuilt from scratch each tick. When a big event fires — a star injury, a prospect breakout, an owner mandate change, a rival blockbuster — a disruption handler registry computes a PlanDelta and applies it in place, then cascades follow-up triggers (rebuild the cap board after a target is removed, etc.). In-season cap and roster events fire fireReplanTrigger directly.

triggerthe plan

Its other headline path is the weekly trade cycle, where perception asymmetry turns into real value transfer: a low-talentEvaluation GM over-perceives the other team’s assets and under-perceives its own, so it accepts deals that quietly bleed value to the sharper GM.

coach lib/brain/coach

↑ back to cards

The coach perceives players through a computeSystemFitRating lens — not raw skill, but fit to its system — so a defensive coach down-rates a one-way scorer. From that assessment, buildLineup assembles four forward lines, three D pairs, PP/PK units, and a starting goalie, balancing chemistry; recommendScratches flags underperformers, which the GM must approve before they sit. A staff review separately grades the assistants.

owner lib/brain/owner

↑ back to cards

The owner perceives wins and losses clearly but roster quality poorly (fixed low skill ~30). Each month evaluateOwnerBlame attributes losing to the coach, the GM, or the roster, and a rolling satisfaction metric is refreshed for the coach and GM from record-vs-expected, recent losses, streaks, and average player satisfaction. Blame and firing both debounce — a target must persist across two cycles before it sticks — which suppresses single-cycle RNG noise. The owner also meddles (vetoes, forced signings) and sets the contend-vs-rebuild mandate.

player lib/brain/player

↑ back to cards

computeSelfAssessment rates a player’s own skill with ego-driven inflation or deflation; computeSatisfaction blends ice time, team success, coach fit, city, and contract fairness into a PlayerSatisfaction. Low satisfaction plus an inflated self-view drives contract demands and a wantsTradeRequest flag — the seed for a trade-request story arc. Separate paths assign the player’s archetype and decide retirement.

scout lib/brain/scout

↑ back to cards

buildIndividualReport produces a per-prospect read — ceiling and current skill, each blurred by Gaussian noise scaled by the scout’s accuracy and region specialty — and buildDraftBoard aggregates a team’s scouts into a ranked board. The board is the GM’s perceived view of the draft class, so a weak scouting department systematically mis-ranks prospects and the GM drafts the wrong player believing it’s right.

agent lib/brain/agent

↑ back to cards

The narrowest brain: pickAgentDecision chooses a negotiation stance for a player’s contract when a story arc surfaces a decision (e.g. agent_negotiation_stance). Like the other roles’ arc deciders it tries real logic for known decision ids — scoring options with the shared scoreToWeightedPick / personalityFactor primitives — and falls back to a weighted pick for the rest, tagging the result ai_brain or placeholder for audit.

Data it owns

Brain state is its own; world state is borrowed. These are the types lib/brain defines and persists across the season.

BrainStateCross-season state: strategic plans, disruption logs, and walkdown (replan) audit events.
SeasonBrainState · TeamBrainStatePer-season working state: assessment caches, satisfaction, blame, and firing & trade records.
GMAssessment · CoachAssessment · OwnerAssessmentCached weekly perception snapshots (7-day TTL via AssessmentCache), one per role per team.
StrategicPlanThe GM’s perceived 5-year plan: CapBoard, OrgDepthChart, PipelineMap, ProScoutingBoard, draft board.
TradeProposal · TradeEvaluationThe trade negotiation loop: proposals, perceived valuations, accept/reject/counter outcomes.

Reads (never owns): RosterSOA, standings, cap state from lib/sim; Franchise/Person from lib/world; profiles & personality from lib/profiles.

Domain

Six role-based AI brains that perceive the league through their own noisy lens and decide what their franchise does.

OwnsAll strategic planning, player evaluation, satisfaction, blame, and arc decisions for the GM, Coach, Owner, Player, Scout, and Agent roles.
WhyThere is no god-mode AI. Each role sees a skill-scaled, noise-distorted version of the truth, so a weak GM genuinely mis-values its own roster — and that is where franchise dysfunction comes from.
RuleBrains never mutate world state. They read the world and return decisions; lib/sim executes them. That one-way contract keeps the season deterministic.
The load-bearing idea
“Skill is perception quality, not decision quality.”

Every brain runs the same perceive → assess → decide pipeline. What differs by skill is the noise added during perception: evaluatePlayer adds Gaussian error scaled by (100 − evaluatorSkill). High-skill GMs see clearly; low-skill GMs over-value other teams’ assets and under-value their own.

Internal architecture

perceive → decide

Every brain runs the same pipeline. The trigger engine reads world state, the perception layer distorts it by skill, role assessments cache a weekly snapshot, and decisions are handed to sim to execute.

World state (read-only)PerceptionThis domainsim (executes)
☉The GM brain is the one to know: it maintains a perceived 5-year StrategicPlan (CapBoard, OrgDepthChart, PipelineMap, ProScoutingBoard) that a disruption handler registry rewrites when big events fire — a star injury, a prospect breakout, an owner mandate change, a rival blockbuster.

Data it owns

Brain state is its own; world state is borrowed. These are the types lib/brain defines and persists across the season.

BrainStateCross-season state: strategic plans, disruption logs, and walkdown (replan) audit events.
SeasonBrainState · TeamBrainStatePer-season working state: assessment caches, satisfaction, blame, and firing & trade records.
GMAssessment · CoachAssessment · OwnerAssessmentCached weekly perception snapshots (7-day TTL via AssessmentCache), one per role per team.
StrategicPlanThe GM’s perceived 5-year plan: CapBoard, OrgDepthChart, PipelineMap, ProScoutingBoard, draft board.
TradeProposal · TradeEvaluationThe trade negotiation loop: proposals, perceived valuations, accept/reject/counter outcomes.

Reads (never owns): RosterSOA, standings, cap state from lib/sim; Franchise/Person from lib/world; profiles & personality from lib/profiles.

Key flow: a mid-season trade

weekly

The clearest path through the domain — the weekly trade cycle, where perception asymmetry turns into real value transfer between teams.

trigger

A low-talentEvaluation GM over-perceives the other team’s assets and under-perceives its own, so it accepts deals that quietly transfer value to the sharper GM. executeAcceptedTrade swaps the players, rebuilds both RosterSOAs and cap states, and fires a replan on both teams.
